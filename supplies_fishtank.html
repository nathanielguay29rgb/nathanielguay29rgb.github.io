Fish Tank Supplies

This page will outline the supplies that you need for your aquarium, as well as some of the science behind why you need these things.

  • Tank (Holds fish/invertebrates, remember to do research on the capacity of your tank to know how many and which species to include)
  • Sturdy Aquarium Stand (Supports the aquarium, make sure that it is rated for the weight of your aquarium and consider placing a leveling mat on top)
  • Filter: Sponge, hang on the back, or canister (Clears tank water and allows surface area for the beneficial bacteria in your ecosystem to grow)
  • Heater (Heats water, do research to learn about the temperature ranges of your desired species)
  • Lid (If your species are prone to jumping, get a custom lid or make one at home)
  • Substrate: Gravel, sand, or aquasoil (Do research on the substrate that your species prefer, and think about aquasoil or root tabs if you are including live plants)
  • Plants (Helps with water parameters and provided hiding spots for animals)
  • Thermometer (Confirms aquarium temperature)
  • Airline tubing, air pump, and check valve (These add-ons are necessary if you are planning on using a sponge filter)
  • Airstone (Quiets sponge filter and oxygenates water - optional)
  • Test kit (Tests water)

Cycling Process

There are many cycling methods, but all of them have the same goal: Grow beneficial bacteria in order to convert ammonia to nitrite to nitrate (nitrogen cycle). This process typically takes 2 weeks to 2 months, and can be slowed down by an acidic/low PH. You can either use live fish or a bottled solution as your ammonia source, and can optionally use bottled bacteria to start a colony faster (although some bottled bacteria strains require specific water parameters).
